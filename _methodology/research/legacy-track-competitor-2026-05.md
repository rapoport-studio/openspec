## 1. Studios / agencies on Legacy / Long-arc / Stewardship themes

### Global / US

**37signals (Chicago, US).** Closest pure-play "built to last" positioning in software. Tagline themes: *"An obligation to independence"*, *"Bury the hustle"*, *"Companies aren't families"* — catalog of 37 signed principles framed as anti-growth-at-all-costs. **25 straight years of profitability, zero debt, privately held, "built to stay rather than exit."** Not a studio for hire — they sell products (Basecamp, HEY, Fizzy, ONCE) and books (REWORK, It Doesn't Have to Be Crazy at Work, Shape Up). Functions as reference manifesto rather than competitor. [source: https://37signals.com/00] [source: https://37signals.com/]

**Mule Design Studio (San Francisco).** Verbatim: *"Last of the independents"*, *"We have been a design consultancy for a very long time."* Sells consulting + workshops (Collaborative Design Research, Conversational Design, six-week Team Research Experience) + books + podcast network. Tagline *"Designed to work."* No pricing disclosed. Positions endurance and ethical practice as identity. [source: https://www.muledesign.com/]

**Pentagram (London / NY, founded 1972).** *"World's largest independent design consultancy"* — 24 partner-owners, 50+ years, no holding-company exits. Heritage-as-positioning by virtue of duration rather than slogan. Not directly competitive with RS scale, but the canonical "we will outlast you" design firm. [source: https://www.pentagram.com/about] [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pentagram_(design_firm)]

**Long Now Foundation (San Francisco, founded 01996).** Not an agency — a cultural institution. *"Foster long-term thinking and responsibility… the framework of the next 10,000 years."* Publishes *Pace Layers* journal. Useful as the cultural reference point any "legacy-positioned" studio implicitly draws from. [source: https://longnow.org/] [source: https://longnow.org/ideas/about/long-term-thinking/]

**Stripe Press (San Francisco, since 2018).** *"Ideas for progress"* — books "about economic, technological, and societal advancement." Strategic asset, not a service. Demonstrates the "publish a durable canon" move as positioning lever. [source: https://press.stripe.com/]

**INDIO (US, founders Michael / Christy / Alex / Cassie).** Verbatim: *"Your brand is the one thing with the power to outlast you, your office, and even the product you sell."* Sells "interior identity design" — architecture + branding + interiors + strategy. Frames choices as having *"enduring meaning"* vs. trend-chasing. [source: https://www.indio.design/blog/a-new-design-studios-founding-manifesto]

**Heirloom Agency (San Francisco).** Branding agency operating literally under "heirloom" framing — durability as core promise. Reviews on Clutch. [source: https://www.heirloomagency.com/] [source: https://clutch.co/profile/heirloom-agency]

**"Digital heirloom marketing" framework.** Emerging vocabulary cluster — products and brands "designed to remain useful and meaningful for fifty years"; supports include *"full-data export in standard formats… documented shutdown or migration plan… inheritance features (legacy contact or successor access)."* Concept-level, not a single firm — but the language is escaping. [source: https://www.influencers-time.com/digital-heirloom-marketing-redefines-brand-value-in-2026/]

### UK / EU

**Set Studio (UK, Andy Bell).** Tagline *"Move fast (without breaking things)"* and *"saving budgets in the long term"*; *"over 50 years of combined, elite level experience."* Methodology-as-positioning (public Eleventy-from-scratch course, Piccalilli publishing arm). Not "legacy" per se — frames durability as **resource-efficiency and accessibility** rather than generational stewardship. No pricing disclosed. [source: https://set.studio/] [source: https://piccalil.li/author/andy-bell/]

**Studio Dumbar (Rotterdam, since 1977).** Heritage-by-duration; visual identity practice. Not "legacy" in copy but in operational fact. [source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Studio_Dumbar]

### MD / RO / IL

**No verified hits.** Cross-referencing baseline files: no Moldovan, Romanian, or Israeli studio surveyed in the existing landscape research positions explicitly on legacy / durability / generational / stewardship themes. Closest verbal adjacency:

- **Linnify (Cluj, RO):** *"long-term partnerships… stays by your side, focused on trust, results, and continued success."* This is partnership-longevity, not legacy-longevity. Sells fullstack dev / AI / cloud / UX to scale-ups + enterprise. No pricing. [source: https://www.linnify.com/]
- **WEBTISAN (Bucharest):** self-describes as *"digital artisans"*, *"new generation of highly skilled artisans"* — craftsmanship framing, no longevity claim. [source: https://www.behance.net/webtisan]
- **Indrivo (Chișinău / RO):** local-owned, custom dev, government / EU institutional work — durability-by-client-type, no slogan. [source: https://indrivo.com/about/our-story]

**Gap finding:** the legacy / long-arc wedge is **structurally unoccupied** in MD/RO/IL and thinly occupied in EU. Closest precedent vocabulary is concentrated in US/UK.

## 2. "Studio for second-time founders"

**No studio uses this phrasing as headline.** The angle is owned by **communities and venture studios**, not service studios:

- **South Park Commons (San Francisco).** *"A home for the most talented technologists and founders figuring out what's next"*, *"helps you go from -1 to 0"*, *"turn the illegible into the inevitable."* Two products: 6-month Residency, Founder Fellowship (up to $1M investment, 1:1 partner mentorship, small cohorts, no set deadlines). Members include "exited founders and highly-cited researchers." Equity terms not public. 1,000+ members over a decade, 250+ companies started. [source: https://www.southparkcommons.com/] [source: https://www.southparkcommons.com/community]

- **Post-Exit Founders (Israel-anchored).** Community founded by Barak Kaufman. March 2025 delegation brought 45 founders with combined $5B in exits to Israel. *"When you exit a company, it's a surprisingly lonely feeling."* Community, not studio. [source: https://themedialine.org/by-region/israel/post-exit-founders-eye-new-ventures-in-israel-amid-varied-economic-forecast/]

- **The Flywheel (Europe).** Venture studio "backed by… US-based entrepreneurs with European roots, tech founders who have scaled and exited startups." Generalist co-creation model. [source: https://tech.eu/2025/12/04/entrepreneurs-launch-european-ai-venture-studio/]

- **Hexa (ex-eFounders, Paris).** SaaS-focused venture studio, 30+ companies launched (Spendesk, Aircall, Front). Equity-co-founder model, not service. [source: https://www.hexa.com/blog/news/start-up-studio]

- **Chapter 2 (UK, Steven Bartlett).** Talent ecosystem framing — embedded specialists. Adjacent, not direct. [source: https://www.chapter2.group/]

**Implication:** "second-time founder service studio with 6-month engagement floor" is an **uncontested copy wedge**. Adjacent players are equity / community plays, not paid-engagement plays.

## 3. Knowledge stewardship / IP packaging

**The vocabulary cluster exists but is dominated by self-help / playbooks, not service studios:**

- **Consulting Success (Michael Zipursky).** Productization guides — pattern: $2,000-$2,500 diagnostic → $20,000+ engagements. [source: https://www.consultingsuccess.com/consultants-guide-to-productization]
- **Adventures in Products** — "Productise Your Expertise" program. [source: https://adventuresinproducts.com/products/productise-your-expertise/]
- **Philip Morgan** — "expertise into IP" methodology podcast / program. [source: https://www.productizeandscale.com/expertise-into-ip-philip-morgan/]
- **Thinkific, Wayfront, QuantumByte** — productized-consulting how-tos. [source: https://www.thinkific.com/blog/productization-consulting-service/] [source: https://wayfront.com/blog/productized-consulting] [source: https://quantumbyte.ai/articles/productizing-consulting]

**No verified studio sells "we package consultants' IP into durable digital products" as a flagship engagement.** Course platforms (Thinkific, Teachable, Maven) sit downstream; book publishers (Stripe Press) sit at the corporate scale. The boutique service tier between them — "expert → durable IP-product, white-glove" — is structurally empty in the regions surveyed.

## 4. Research-grade tooling for solo researchers / indie scientists

**The space is dominated by individuals and meta-science funders, not service studios:**

- **Andy Matuschak (independent, US).** *"Creating user interfaces that expand what people can think and do."* Patreon-funded since 2019; late-2025 on "para-academic leave" co-building **Pico**, *"a conservatory for human attention"* with Taylor Rogalski — moving to startup form. Single-person reference of the archetype. [source: https://andymatuschak.org/] [source: https://www.patreon.com/quantumcountry/about]
- **Microsoft Research — Tools for Thought.** Corporate research division publishing on cognition + GenAI. Not a service. [source: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/project/tools-for-thought/]
- **Protocol Labs Research.** $5M+ RFP-driven grants program for network-protocol research. Funder, not builder-for-hire. [source: https://research.protocol.ai/] [source: https://github.com/protocol/research-grants]
- **Astera Institute / Convergent Research.** Focused Research Organization (FRO) movement — funding metascience, not building tools for individuals. [source: https://bigthink.com/the-future/progress-conference-2025-metascience/]
- **Ink & Switch.** Research lab publishing "Slow Software" — closest "lab building durable software primitives" reference. [source: https://inkandswitch.com/slow-software.html]

**Implication:** boutique service-studio offering "research-grade tools for solo experts" has no verified incumbent in MD/RO/IL/EU. Closest analogue is one researcher (Matuschak) self-funding via Patreon, now spinning out a startup.

## 5. Pricing patterns in legacy-positioned services

**Pricing is almost universally hidden in this segment.** Confirmed observations:

| Studio | Pricing disclosure | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Set Studio (UK) | None public | *"fair pricing with lower overheads"* vs traditional agencies [source: https://set.studio/] |
| Mule Design (US) | None public | Workshop catalog implies productized tiers but no $ shown [source: https://www.muledesign.com/] |
| Pentagram | None public | Partner-led custom engagements |
| Linnify (RO) | None public | "long-term partnerships" framing; bands disclosed nowhere [source: https://www.linnify.com/] |
| Indrivo (MD) | None public | Government / institutional |
| Heirloom Agency | None public; Clutch reviews suggest mid-tier branding rates | [source: https://clutch.co/profile/heirloom-agency] |
| 37signals | Sells products, not services | Books $20-40; Basecamp flat $299/mo — not comparable to studio engagements |
| South Park Commons | $1M Fellowship investment; equity terms private | Venture, not engagement [source: https://www.southparkcommons.com/] |
| Productized-consulting reference | $2K-$2.5K diagnostic → $20K+ engagement (Consulting Success) [source: https://www.consultingsuccess.com/consultants-guide-to-productization] |

**Calibration vs Pavel's €50k floor / 6-month minimum:**

- **6-month minimum is legacy-segment-typical.** SPC residency = 6 months. Mule's Team Research Experience = 6 weeks but framed as entry. Shape Up cycles = 6 weeks, six cycles = 9 months. The 6-month window aligns with industry-default for "this is a relationship, not a transaction" framing.
- **€50k floor is mid-tier for legacy-positioned EU / US boutiques.** Baseline `competitor-landscape-synthesis.md` § 7 already brackets RS-adjacent work at €40-250k. €50k sits at the low end of that range and well above CEE bodyshop rates (€15-30k typical project). It is **structurally consistent with legacy positioning** but does not signal premium tier — Pentagram-class is 3-10× higher; Linnify-class is reportedly in the same band (no public confirm).
- **No legacy-positioned studio surveyed publishes a floor publicly.** Disclosure itself would be a differentiator if RS chose to.

## 6. Anti-examples — explicit speed / lean / anti-craft positioning

- **SEM Nexus — "How to Build an AI MVP in 90 Days: The Lean Founder's Blueprint."** Verbatim: *"In the hyper-competitive 2026 AI landscape… speed is your ultimate competitive advantage."* [source: https://semnexus.com/how-to-build-ai-mvp-90-days/]
- **Lean Labs — "loop marketing 90-day roadmap."** [source: https://www.leanlabs.com/blog/how-to-implement-loop-marketing-90-day-roadmap]
- **Growth Method — 30-60-90 day playbooks.** [source: https://growthmethod.com/30-60-90/]
- **Wix Studio blog — "developer velocity."** Velocity as positive-coded virtue. [source: https://www.wix.com/studio/blog/developer-velocity]
- **Solo-AI-builder narrative (DEV.to, crazyburst, etc.).** *"$10K-$100K/month from a bedroom"*; share of US solo-founded startups rose from 22% (2015) to 38% (2024). Cultural counterweight to legacy framing. [source: https://dev.to/dev_tips/the-solo-dev-saas-stack-powering-10kmonth-micro-saas-tools-in-2025-pl7] [source: https://crazyburst.com/ai-saas-solo-founder-success-stories-2026/]

**Implication:** the dominant 2025-26 narrative in the SMB / founder market is **velocity, scrappiness, solo-builder + AI**. Legacy positioning is a counter-narrative wedge — small but coherent, with named anchors (37signals, Mule, Pentagram, Long Now, INDIO).

## 7. Open observations (not recommendations)

- **Cluster density:** ~10 verified studios / institutions worldwide use legacy / long-arc language with any consistency. The cluster is real but small. Existing baseline competitor files identified ~80+ studios across MD/RO/IL/EU; **fewer than 5 of them touch legacy vocabulary.**
- **Vocabulary fragmentation:** "stewardship", "heirloom", "durable", "long arc", "outlast", "calm", "independent", "built to stay" — no consolidated lexicon. Risk + opportunity: positioning on this axis means **defining the term**, not adopting an established one.
- **Geographic concentration:** US-West-Coast (SF/SV) and UK / Netherlands hold most reference points. MD/RO/IL field is open.
- **Service-vs-product split:** the strongest legacy-voice players (37signals, Stripe Press, Long Now) are **product / publishing entities**, not service studios. The service-studio strain (Mule, Pentagram, INDIO) is more diffuse and rarely names the legacy theme as headline copy — they perform it through duration and partner-ownership structure rather than slogan.
- **Second-time-founder × paid-engagement intersection:** verified-empty. SPC, Post-Exit Founders, Flywheel, Hexa all occupy adjacent slots (community / equity / studio-cofounder), none sell a fixed-engagement service to this cohort.
