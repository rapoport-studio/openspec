## 1. Parallel audience-track patterns

The dominant agency pattern is **one site, parallel landing pages with shared services** — same underlying offerings reframed per audience. Real-world examples and structural notes:

- **PetLab**: separate landing pages per pet-owner segment (older owners vs. active-lifestyle). Same product catalog, segment-specific headlines, imagery, offers. [source: https://www.tutorialchip.com/design/ways-use-multi-landing-pages-increase-conversions-different-audience-segments/]
- **Hims & Hers**: multi-landing-page model for diverse customer groups with targeted messaging, visuals, offers. [source: https://www.tutorialchip.com/design/ways-use-multi-landing-pages-increase-conversions-different-audience-segments/]
- **B2B SaaS norm** (cited in SevenAtoms / ConvertFlow): separate landing pages for "marketing agencies, startups, and freelancers" — startup pages emphasize affordability / ease; enterprise pages emphasize scalability / security. [source: https://www.sevenatoms.com/blog/multiple-landing-pages-to-target-different-buyer-personas] [source: https://www.convertflow.com/blog/audience-segmentation]
- **thoughtbot / Startup Stack**: dedicated audience sub-page for startups with discount + resources, separate from the main `/our-company` page. [source: https://thoughtbot.com/our-company/startup-stack]
- **MetaLab `/ai`**: a vertical-specific page parallel to `/work`, with industry-specific framing of the same studio capabilities. [source: https://www.metalab.com/ai]
- **Pentagram `/work`** is organized "by type of client" — explicit client-segment filtering in IA. [source: https://www.pentagram.com/work]
- **Family-office sites** (Pathstone, Rockefeller Capital, ICONIQ, Halstatt, Bessemer Trust) follow a single-narrative homepage rather than parallel tracks; segmentation happens by service page, not audience landing page. [source: https://durkangroup.com/top-10-best-family-office-websites/]

**What differs across tracks** (per ConvertFlow / Apexure / SevenAtoms): headlines, sub-heads, hero imagery, pain-point framing, social proof selection, and CTA verb — but not the underlying service catalog or pricing. Intake is sometimes shared, sometimes split (see §3). [source: https://www.convertflow.com/blog/audience-segmentation] [source: https://www.sevenatoms.com/blog/multiple-landing-pages-to-target-different-buyer-personas]

## 2. Narrative-led pages for long-arc / generational buyers

Across the family-office / private-wealth set, the dominant homepage sequence is **Hero → Philosophy / Values → Team & People → Service Pillars → Insights / Content → Soft CTA** — explicitly NOT lead-gen-first. [source: https://durkangroup.com/top-10-best-family-office-websites/] [source: https://www.tbhcreative.com/blog/family-office-websites-and-private-equity-web-design/]

Concrete section orders observed:

- **Rockefeller Capital Management**: Vision → Mission → Purpose → Testimonials. [source: https://durkangroup.com/top-10-best-family-office-websites/]
- **Crosby Advisors**: Team → Philosophy → Process → Personalized Guidance. [source: https://durkangroup.com/top-10-best-family-office-websites/]
- **Gresham Partners**: Core Values → Value Proposition. [source: https://durkangroup.com/top-10-best-family-office-websites/]
- **Matter Family Office**: Family Stories → Challenges → Solutions. [source: https://durkangroup.com/top-10-best-family-office-websites/]
- **White Oak Enterprises**: Simplicity Messaging → News / Insights. [source: https://durkangroup.com/top-10-best-family-office-websites/]

Verbatim guidance for UHNW / long-arc audiences: "UHNW buyers are bombarded daily with opportunities, so standing out requires something truly distinctive — the story, brand partnerships and lifestyle promise must resonate on a deeply personal level." [source: https://www.inman.com/2025/10/05/how-to-craft-bespoke-marketing-for-ultra-high-net-worth-buyers/]

TBH Creative on family-office IA: "From the homepage, visitors gain direct insight into partners, philosophy, and personalities… transparency allowing users to envision themselves working with the team." [source: https://www.tbhcreative.com/blog/family-office-websites-and-private-equity-web-design/]

## 3. Intake form — separate vs. shared

**Operator argument for separate intake** (Pete Bowen, paraphrasing): a contact page is the "landing page of last resort" — "a generic, undifferentiated place where your site visitor can connect with you," whereas a landing-page form is "tuned to exactly what is going on in the mind of the prospect." [source: https://pete-bowen.com/landing-pages-click-to-call-or-contact-form] [source: https://blog.austinlawrence.com/blog/theres-a-big-difference-between-contact-us-and-a-landing-page-and-its-all-about-lead-generation-performance]

**Operator argument for shared with project intake as step 2**: SEOptimer's agency-intake guide describes a "3 or 4 part new client intake process": initial contact (shared form / email) → project intake form (segment-specific) → proposal → kickoff. The split happens *after* first contact, not on the landing page. [source: https://www.seoptimer.com/blog/project-intake-form/]

**Conversion lift cited**: "personalized pages lifted conversions 68%"; dedicated landing pages convert 2-3× higher than generic product pages for matched traffic. [source: https://firework.com/blog/landing-page-statistics] [source: https://landerlab.io/blog/landing-page-conversion-rate]

- "Drop a message to connect" — Durkan Group review of family-office sites [source: https://durkangroup.com/top-10-best-family-office-websites/]
- "Begin Your Tax Planning", "Start Here" — recommended verbs in CPA / advisor CTA writeups [source: https://blog.twentyoverten.com/4-creative-ways-financial-advisors-can-use-calls-to-action-examples/] [source: https://www.cpasitesolutions.com/cpa-websites/2024/11/call-to-action/]

- "Request introduction" / "Apply" specifically are **not documented in surfaced wealth-advisor research** — these are agency-side norms (e.g. Basecamp's "Apply to work with us") but no public conversion data was found.

## 5. Nav placement

The canonical authority is **Nielsen Norman Group**, which lists 5 reasons to AVOID top-nav audience splits:

1. "Users don't know which group to choose."
2. Ambiguous labels (about-that-group vs. for-that-group).
3. "Forcing people to self-identify creates an additional step and takes people out of their task mindset."
4. "Users feel anxious that the information they see might be incomplete or incorrect."
5. Overlapping content causes "pogo-sticking between sections."

NN/G's exception clause: **"Role-based navigation works best when the content is unique to that audience group. There should be enough information specifically for that group to justify its own section."** [source: https://www.nngroup.com/articles/audience-based-navigation/]

NN/G's recommended placement when criteria are met: **utility nav**, not main nav — "Role-based sections can be given less priority with their placement in the utility navigation." [source: https://www.nngroup.com/articles/audience-based-navigation/]

## 6. Internationalization timing

**Launch-in-one-language-first is the documented norm** for early-stage B2B sites:

- Smartling / Centus: "Building a quick site in the target language of the market you plan to enter can help you see the traffic and engagement you're getting to decide if the ROI is strong enough to invest in that market." [source: https://centus.com/blog/saas-localization]
- Crowdin: "Marketing teams should prioritize content for localization rather than trying to translate every element simultaneously, focusing on high-impact content first." [source: https://crowdin.com/blog/multilingual-marketing]
- TransPerfect: "Initial results of multilingual marketing implementation will be visible in 3-6 months, but fully integrating the multilingual marketing strategy takes more than 12-18 months." [source: https://globallink.transperfect.com/blog/multilingual-marketing-how-to-reach-foreign-language-audiences]
- Critical caveat (Lokalise / SimpleLocalize): retrofitting localization is "messy and pricey" — keep i18n keys and routing in place from day 1 even if only English ships. [source: https://lokalise.com/blog/multilingual-websites/] [source: https://simplelocalize.io/blog/posts/plan-first-5-languages-localization/]

## 7. Conversion data — audience-specific vs. generic

- "Businesses that create well-optimized landing pages can experience up to a 220% boost in conversion rates compared to generic web pages." [source: https://firework.com/blog/landing-page-statistics]
- "Dedicated landing pages convert 2-3x higher than generic product pages for paid traffic because they match the ad's specific message and remove navigation distractions." [source: https://landerlab.io/blog/landing-page-conversion-rate]
- "A B2B page that dynamically changes its content to show industry-specific messaging… consistently outperforms generic pages by more than 30% in engagement and conversions." [source: https://genesysgrowth.com/blog/landing-page-conversion-stats-for-marketing-leaders]
- "Personalized pages lifted conversions 68%." [source: https://firework.com/blog/landing-page-statistics]
- "Case studies, specific client results, and named testimonials near the CTA consistently outperform generic agency pages by 2-3×." [source: https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/landing-page-conversion-study-2000-pages-tested-2026]
- Marketing Week survey cited by Unbounce: 91% of marketers consider behavioral segmentation "the most effective kind of segmentation." [source: https://unbounce.com/campaign-strategy/behavioral-segmentation/]

**Caveat**: no surfaced data isolates *audience-track parallel pages* (e.g., `/founders` vs `/legacy`) from *ad-campaign landing pages*. The 2-3× and 30-220% numbers are aggregated across both. Public data specifically comparing a `/founders`-style audience track to a generic `/services` page on the same agency site was **not found** in this round.
