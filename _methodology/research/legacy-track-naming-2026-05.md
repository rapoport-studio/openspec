## 1. EN connotations: "Legacy" in tech buyer minds

**Strong evidence the term is overloaded with "old / replace me" in tech contexts:**

- **Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2024:** Technical debt (which the survey ties directly to legacy systems) is the #1 developer frustration at **62%** — "twice as much as the second- and third-most frustrating problems." [source: https://stackoverflow.blog/2024/08/06/2024-developer-survey/]
- **2025 Stack Overflow Survey:** Frustration shifted to AI ("almost right but not quite" at 66%), but legacy/tech-debt remained a dominant theme. [source: https://stackoverflow.blog/2025/12/29/developers-remain-willing-but-reluctant-to-use-ai-the-2025-developer-survey-results-are-here/]
- **Gartner Peer Community survey:** 93% of dev teams report active technical debt; "architecture debt" most-cited. [source: https://www.zartis.com/why-2025-is-the-year-tech-debt-becomes-a-strategic-risk/]
- **Bospar PR (industry marketing firm):** "When it comes to the universe of tech, the term 'legacy' carries a truly negative connotation, particularly with analysts, influencers and, most importantly, customers… tantamount to saying it is irrelevant, a dinosaur." [source: https://bospar.com/the-taint-of-legacy-positioning/]
- **MC Press / "Stop Saying Legacy":** Industry argument that marketers weaponized "legacy" as a wedge term to sell replacements. [source: https://www.mcpressonline.com/programming/rpg/stop-saying-qlegacyq]
- **Rocket Software blog ("Why 'Legacy' Shouldn't Be a Bad Word"):** Confirms the term is widely treated as pejorative even by vendors trying to reclaim it. [source: https://blog.rocketsoftware.com/2020/10/why-legacy-shouldnt-be-a-bad-word/]
- **innoQ blog "What's in a name: Legacy":** "To any person involved with technology, the word legacy has a profoundly negative meaning… stands for an old, somehow bad piece of software." [source: https://www.innoq.com/en/blog/2021/07/whats-in-a-name-legacy/]

**Counter-evidence — non-tech buyers read it positively:**

- innoQ same article: "For most people out there, the word 'legacy' typically has a positive connotation" — generational / inheritance reading dominates outside IT. [source: https://www.innoq.com/en/blog/2021/07/whats-in-a-name-legacy/]

## 3. RU market — "Наследие" vs "Наследство"

- **"Наследие"** = cultural / intellectual legacy; solemn register; tied to world-significance objects, ideas, achievements. Not directly tied to death. [source: https://paronim-k-slovu.ru/наследие-наследство]
- **"Наследство"** = property / inheritance after the owner's death; everyday register; **direct death association.** [source: https://ru.wiktionary.org/wiki/наследство]
- Russian marketing literature treats "наследие" as a **positive strategic brand tool** — connecting brand to cultural heritage drives trust and loyalty. [source: https://www.sostav.ru/publication/kod-uspekha-kak-proshloe-rabotaet-na-brend-segodnya-80901.html]
- HSE (Higher School of Economics) marketing blog confirms heritage-coded brands enjoy highest trust in Russian-speaking urban markets. [source: https://top100person.ru/news/lichnyy-brend-i-nasledie-kak-postroit-brend-kotoryy-perezhivet-vas/]

**Net: "Legacy"-as-English-loanword and "Наследие" both read positively in RU; "Наследство" carries death weight, avoid.**

## 4. RO market — "Moștenire"

- **"Moștenire" and "succesiune" are legally equivalent** — both refer to the post-death transfer of assets to heirs. Romanian legal / notarial context dominates the term. [source: https://bundeangajat.olx.ro/succesiunea-mostenirea-care-sunt-mostenitorii-legali-cum-se-impart-bunurile-acte-necesare-si-taxe/] [source: https://lege5.ro/gratuit/he3dqnbq/despre-succesiuni-codul-civil]
- Dale Carnegie Romania uses "Moștenire" for its "Heritage" page — corporate / positive use exists. [source: https://www.dalecarnegie.com/ro/approach/heritage]
- Business-press example: Start-up.ro feature on Alina Mecherel uses "moștenire" for family-business inheritance — positive framing. [source: https://start-up.ro/antreprenoarele-she-s-next-alina-mecherel-lemnul-continua-sa-spuna-o-poveste]

**Net: "Moștenire" in RO is dominated by death / notarial register. Business usage exists but is the minority frame; reader's first association is succession after death.**

## 6. Internal-term-collides-with-brand-name precedent

No agency-side case study surfaced in this pass. Adjacent precedent: tech vendors actively trying to reclaim "legacy" (Rocket Software [https://blog.rocketsoftware.com/2020/10/why-legacy-shouldnt-be-a-bad-word/]) do so without reporting internal grep pain. The closest documented pattern is **terminology splits** — "Heritage" (positive, branding) vs "Legacy" (pejorative, engineering) — where firms deliberately separate registers. Tavern Agency explicitly does this: "Modern Heritage" externally, with no overlap to engineering vocab. [source: https://tavern.agency/]

## 7. Summary of risk surface (no recommendation)

- **EN tech-adjacent buyers**: 62% top-frustration data + multiple industry pieces explicitly flag "legacy" as pejorative for IT buyers.
- **RU**: "Наследие" = clean positive. "Наследство" = death-tied, avoid.
- **RO**: "Moștenire" = legally and culturally death / succession-coded; business use is minority frame.
- **Crowded EN field**: ~7 live "Legacy [X]" studios already exist, mostly mid-market branding shops — no premium tech-tier occupant.
- **Available alternatives with weak claim**: Steward, Perennial, Long arc, Long view, Continuity, Enduring (no live agency owns these).
- **Internal collision (`legacy_promoted`, "Legacy six", "legacy tokens")**: no documented agency precedent; closest analogue is the Heritage / Legacy register split (Tavern, Untold) — they avoid the collision by using "Heritage" externally and reserving "legacy" for engineering register.
