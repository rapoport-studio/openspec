## 1. Who actually buys "build for the long arc" in 2025-2026

Verified buyer profiles:

- **Second-time founders (Israel-heavy, cyber-heavy):** Israeli mega-seed rounds in 2024-2025 are "overwhelmingly associated with second-time founders, often in cybersecurity, who already have strong global networks." Examples: Ofir Ehrlich (Eon, $4B), veterans behind Tenzai ($75M seed). [source: https://www.calcalistech.com/ctechnews/article/bkerbckewg]
- **Psychological shift in repeat founders:** Blume Ventures interview with a second-time founder: "you find yourself a lot more comfortable and at peace with the process… a certain level of stress but also a lot of conscious joy." [source: https://blume.vc/commentaries/confessions-of-a-second-time-founder]
- **Midlife "generativity" cohort:** Academic + practitioner consensus that midlife pivot is driven by "phase of generativity… shift from a focus on personal achievement to finding meaning through contributions to others and the legacy they hope to leave behind, with less to prove and more to give." Activity peaks in mid-adulthood (inverted-U). [source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S088390262500076X] [source: https://www.nextavenue.org/how-entrepreneurship-can-bloom-in-midlife-and-later/]
- **Age band:** Inverted-U on entrepreneurship × age, peak in mid-adulthood (roughly 35-55); ~20-25% of US retirees working in 2024 (signal that "later-life founder" is a real cohort, not anecdote). [source: https://www.nextavenue.org/how-entrepreneurship-can-bloom-in-midlife-and-later/]

## 2. Vocabulary that resonates (verified, with URLs)

- **"Craft will be the new luxury"** — Bürocratik, on staying small + craft in the AI era: "in the Artificial Intelligence era, craft will be the new luxury — and we're all in." Also: "long-term partner… whether it's for the next few months or years." [source: https://www.burocratik.com/studios]
- **"Long-term partnerships," "40 year heritage," "intelligent craft"** — Edenspiekermann about page: "independent ownership and 40 year heritage… establishing long term partnerships… human-centred thinking meets intelligent craft." [source: https://www.edenspiekermann.com/about/us]
- **"Built to stay, not exit"** — 37signals/Basecamp positioning: "profitable for 25 straight years, with zero debt, and built to stay, not exit"; "Small is Not a Stepping Stone." [source: https://icopilots.com/37signals-the-microgiant/] [source: https://37signals.com/podcast/why-grow/]
- **"Stewardship," "future generations"** — WEF 2025: "stewardship concept… build value for future generations, employees, customers and investors." [source: https://www.weforum.org/stories/2025/09/stewardship-businesses-future-generations/]
- **"Patient capital," "long-view leadership"** — Calm Capital: "steady companies deserve patient capital and principled stewardship." [source: https://www.calmcapital.com/why-patient-capital-still-wins/]
- **"Endure beyond trends," "form follows clarity"** — Craftsmen Studio. [source: https://craftsmen.studio/]
- **"Guaranteed human"** — iHeartMedia tagline as anti-AI-slop positioning; 90% of their listeners want media made by humans. [source: https://digiday.com/marketing/backlash-grows-against-ai-slop-but-marketers-remain-unfazed/]
- **"Authenticity premium"** — research-backed framing that AI-generated emotional copy triggers "moral disgust" and weaker purchase intent. [source: https://www.koinsights.com/the-authenticity-premium-why-consumers-are-rejecting-ai-generated-content/]
- **"Specialist, not generalist"** — Promethean 2025 report: 84% of agencies now identify as specialists; specialists outperformed generalists on margins. [source: https://prometheanresearch.com/2025-digital-agency-industry-report/]

## 3. Vocabulary that repels (with evidence)

- **"Move fast and break things"** — explicitly retired by serious commentators. HBR: "The Era of 'Move Fast and Break Things' Is Over." LeadDev, Trellis, IEEE T&S all argue the playbook "no longer serves innovators." [source: https://hbr.org/2019/01/the-era-of-move-fast-and-break-things-is-over] [source: https://leaddev.com/velocity/why-you-shouldnt-move-fast-and-break-things] [source: https://trellis.net/article/its-time-to-ditch-the-move-fast-and-break-things-innovation-playbook/]
- **"AI-powered" / generic "AI" claims** — mentions of "AI slop" up 9× in 2025; negative sentiment hit 54% in October 2025; Merriam-Webster 2025 Word of the Year. [source: https://www.euronews.com/next/2025/12/28/2025-was-the-year-ai-slop-went-mainstream-is-the-internet-ready-to-grow-up-now] [source: https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/ai-backlash-2025]
- **"Magic button" / "10x" / hypergrowth framing** — Joshua Thompson 2025: users "prefer practical tools over magic buttons." 37signals' brand is built explicitly against "hypergrowth" and "valuation/exit" framing. [source: https://joshthompson.co.uk/ai/ai-slop-backlash-tools-over-magic-buttons-2025/] [source: https://icopilots.com/37signals-the-microgiant/]
- **"Minimum viable product"** — being displaced by "minimum virtuous product" framing in innovation discourse. [source: https://trellis.net/article/its-time-to-ditch-the-move-fast-and-break-things-innovation-playbook/]

Caveat: "growth hacking" specifically — could not find a 2024-2025 source explicitly documenting buyer disgust with that phrase. Treat as intuition, not verified.

## 4. Geographic dimension — MD/RO vs IL/EU

- **Moldova: high risk aversion, low credit.** Moldova's loan-to-GDP is ~24% (2024) — "signalling that many firms and consumers are not accessing bank credit, suggesting significant risk aversion in the business sector." Plus persistent "lack of effective and equitable implementation of laws." [source: https://www.coface.com/news-economy-and-insights/business-risk-dashboard/country-risk-files/moldova-republic-of]
- **Romania: declining institutional trust, political volatility.** 2024 presidential vote annulled over foreign-interference allegations; "persistent societal polarization, declining trust in institutions." Investment decisions delayed by political uncertainty. [source: https://www.allianz-trade.com/en_global/economic-research/country-reports/Romania.html] [source: https://www.ey.com/en_ro/attractiveness/2024/steadily-navigating-the-currents-how-will-romania-continue-to-win-investors-trust]
- **Family-business succession is a live cultural trigger in RO.** "Family-run businesses form the backbone of many local economies, yet… too many companies put off the conversation [about succession]." Romanian Civil Code forces inheritance share to spouse/children regardless of will — succession is legally unavoidable. [source: https://iorgulescu-legal.ro/business-succession-planning/]
- **Romanian tech wealth is now generational.** Three of the 2024 Forbes-richest Romanians made fortunes in tech (Dines, Stoica, Zaharia) — UiPath's Dines overtook Ion Țiriac. Signals a new "tech-money legacy" cohort that didn't exist a decade ago. [source: https://www.romania-insider.com/uipath-founder-overtakes-ion-tiriac-wealthiest-romanian-forbes-2024]
- **MD/RO ICP-specific cultural triggers:**
  - Risk aversion + low institutional trust → "patient," "durable," "trusted partner" outperforms "disrupt," "fast"
  - Family-succession discourse → "generational," "build something to pass on" lands harder than in US
  - "Education as legacy" framing already in use locally (Cornel Varvara coverage). [source: https://www.romania-insider.com/romanian-entrepreneurs-international-brands]
- **Israel: opposite center of gravity.** Second-time-founder economy is real but oriented to cyber + US-network scaling, not generational/family framing. Legacy-track messaging there reads as "I already did the unicorn lap, now I want to build something I'm proud of" — not "for my children."

## 5. Triggering events (verified)

- **First-exit/failed-exit reflection** — Blume founder describes second venture as "comfortable and at peace… conscious joy." [source: https://blume.vc/commentaries/confessions-of-a-second-time-founder]
- **Career-stage pivot to generativity** — academic literature: midlife shift from achievement to legacy/contribution. [source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S088390262500076X]
- **Failure-recovery** — "Older entrepreneurs who experience higher positive gains after failure are more likely to re-enter the entrepreneurial space quickly." [source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S088390262500076X]
- **Parenthood** — specific founder interview (Gabby Slome, Ollie): had daughter 2017, less than a year after launching company; explicitly cites the rewiring. [source: https://www.entrepreneur.com/leadership/how-i-started-a-business-and-had-a-baby-in-one-year-without/313201]
- **Anti-AI-slop fatigue as a "switch trigger"** for buyers — public sentiment shift in late 2025; brands like iHeartMedia openly repositioning around "guaranteed human." [source: https://digiday.com/marketing/backlash-grows-against-ai-slop-but-marketers-remain-unfazed/]

**Could NOT verify:**

- Specific founder interviews citing "parent death" or "academic burnout" as trigger
- Country-specific data on what percentage of MD/RO founders are second-time
- Verified hostile reaction to "growth hacking" as a phrase (intuition only)

## 6. Options for Pavel (no recommendation)

- **A. Lead with "craft / patient / durable / stewardship" lexicon** — backed by Edenspiekermann, Bürocratik, 37signals, WEF stewardship framing.
- **B. Lead with "second chapter / built to stay" founder-voice** — backed by Blume founder interview + 37signals positioning.
- **C. Lead with "generational / for what comes after you"** — fits RO family-succession reality + WEF "future generations" frame; weaker fit for IL cyber-founder audience.
- **D. Anti-AI-slop "human, by hand" angle** — current, defensible via 2025 backlash data, but risks being a trend-bet that ages fast.
- **Cross-cutting:** explicitly drop "AI-powered," "move fast," "10x," "magic button" from the Legacy Track surface — all three have hard 2024-2025 evidence of backlash among the long-arc audience.
