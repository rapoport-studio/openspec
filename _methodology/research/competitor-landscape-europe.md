## TL;DR — Europe tech market in numbers

- **VC funding flat:** ~$45B in 2024, ~$44B projected 2025 — down ~58% from 2021's ~$106B peak, but stabilized.
- **Ecosystem value:** ~$4T combined public + private = ~15% of EU GDP (4% in 2016); still ~⅓ of US tech market cap.
- **Workforce:** 10M+ ICT specialists in EU (2024), 5.1% of total employment; Germany alone employs 2.3M (22.1% of EU total).
- **Top 3 hubs by VC:** London ($10.8–11B), Paris ($5.9B city / $8.5B France), Munich/Stockholm tied (~$2.4B).
- **AI surged 2025:** $17.5B in 2025 (vs. ~$10B 2024) — first year AI led European VC; Mistral alone raised €1.7B Series C at €11.7B post-money.
- **Deep tech share:** 36% of European VC dollars in 2025 (vs. 19% in 2021); defence tech hit a record $1.6B.
- **Regulatory cliff:** EU AI Act GPAI obligations + penalties live since 2 Aug 2025; full high-risk regime Aug 2026.
- **Tax landscape:** **No EU regime cleanly beats Moldova MITP 7%.** Estonia best for reinvested profit; Cyprus IP Box 2.5%→3% best for IP holding only.
- **Direct competitive set:** real and material — 8–12 studios across London/Paris/Berlin/CEE.
- **CEE pricing wedge is narrower than initially estimated** — Polish/Czech tier-1 (Netguru, STRV) sit at $70–150/hr, same band as RS could charge.

## 2. Tax/relocation landscape — confirmation MD SRL stays

| Country | 2025 reality | Vs MD MITP 7% |
|---|---|---|
| **Moldova MITP** | 7% от оборота, locked to 2035 | baseline |
| **Estonia OÜ** | 0% on retained earnings; 22% on distributions (preferred 14% **abolished Jan 2025**); +2% profit tax 2026–28 | Worse for cash extraction; still good for reinvested profit |
| **Ireland** | 12.5% trading CIT; R&D credit raised to 35% (Budget 2026); KDB IP rate rising 6.25% → 10% | Tightened but durable; worse than MD |
| **Cyprus** | IP Box effective rate **2.5% in 2025**, rising to **3% from Jan 2026** (CIT 12.5% → 15% for OECD Pillar 2) | Comparable for IP holding only |
| **Portugal** | NHR ended Dec 2024; replaced by **IFICI ("NHR 2.0")** — 20% flat on qualifying tech/research income, 10 yrs | Far narrower; programmer freelancers may not qualify |
| **Spain** | **Beckham Law** still active — 24% flat on Spanish income to €600K, 6 yrs; Digital Nomad Visa (€2,850/мес income req.); autónomos now eligible for Beckham (2026 update) | Best for relocating individual founder, not corporate |
| **UK** | 25% main CIT post-Brexit; SEIS/EIS still strong; no special expat regime | EU access gone; worse |
| **Romania** | 16% CIT, 1–3% micro-company; **IT employee income tax exemption killed Jan 2025** | Lost edge vs MD MITP (see [`competitor-landscape-romania.md`](./competitor-landscape-romania.md)) |

**Conclusion:** No EU regime cleanly beats Moldova's MITP 7%. Estonia OÜ remains best for reinvested-profit tech holdings; Cyprus IP Box best for licensing IP. **MD SRL remains optimal corporate vehicle.**

## 3. Operating a Moldovan SRL across EU (2025 reality)

- **Frictionless:** EUR invoicing via Wise/Revolut Business; B2B services to EU clients use **reverse charge VAT** — Moldovan SRL never registers for EU VAT for pure B2B consulting/dev/design.
- **Banking:** Wise Business + Revolut Business cover EUR/GBP IBANs; full EU bank accounts (commercial banks) require an EU entity or EU-resident beneficial owner.
- **OSS does NOT apply** — OSS is B2C-only; B2B services use reverse charge.
- **GDPR:** applies extraterritorially. DPO mandatory only if core activity = large-scale monitoring or special-category data; RS doesn't trigger it. The 250-employee threshold concerns *records of processing*, not DPO.
- **EU AI Act:** applies to anyone placing AI systems on EU market regardless of HQ — Moldovan SRL building EU-facing AI tools is in scope from Aug 2025 (GPAI/transparency) and Aug 2026 (high-risk).
- **Data residency:** sector-specific (health, finance) — most studio/SaaS work fine outside EU.

### Romanian (= EU) citizenship — what it actually unlocks

- **Personal mobility:** Schengen + right to live/work in any EU country without permits — practical for on-site engagements.
- **Romanian PFA registration:** flat 10% income tax + 25% CAS + 10% CASS on capped bases; **VAT-exempt up to RON 395,000 (~€78K)** as of Sep 2025 (raised from RON 300K).
- **EU Small Business Scheme:** Romanian PFA can claim VAT exemption in *other* EU states up to **€100K EU-wide turnover** (Directive 2020/285).
- **Banking:** retail/business banking opens up across EU; SEPA-native; easier merchant/Stripe access (some Stripe markets still don't accept Moldova).
- **What does NOT change automatically:** Moldovan SRL still operates as before; reverse-charge B2B already worked. **Citizenship adds personal optionality + lightweight EU billing layer (PFA)** alongside the SRL — does not replace it.

## 6. CEE pricing reality — critical correction

This is the most important quantitative correction from this research.

| Sector | Rate range |
|---|---|
| Phantom-tier London | $200-350/hr blended |
| Paris/Berlin Tier-1 | $150-250/hr |
| **STRV (Prague), Netguru (Poland)** | **$70-150/hr** — premium CEE |
| Apptension, Brainhub (PL) | $50-100/hr |
| Ukrainian shops | $35-80/hr |
| Tel Aviv senior product | $120-180/hr |
| Romania boutique | $100-149/hr Clutch median |

**If RS prices senior IC at $120/hr+:** we're **at the same tier as STRV and Netguru**, not cheaper. **CEE-wedge holds vs Phantom-tier London but NOT vs Polish/Czech tier-1 purely on price.**

**Implication:** can't sell on "cheap CEE alternative". Must compete on **vertical depth + AI-native posture + founder-direct + diaspora-bilingual access**. Four wedge axes — price is a fifth, not primary.

## 7. Where the gap is in Europe — narrowest of four regions

Honest assessment: **Europe is design-mature; gap is the narrowest of all four regions surveyed.** The only defensible white space:

1. **Boutique studios that ship their own AI tooling alongside services as peer-products.** Mozza and Maison Meta are early peers, but **neither publishes an open spec-authoring system (Muse) or autonomous dev pipeline (Forge)**. The studio-as-software-company posture is **not yet crowded in Europe**. Linnify has Linny but keeps it private. **This is RS's single most defensible wedge.**
2. **Multilingual EN/RO/RU-capable boutique connecting CEE founder networks to Western capital** — but narrower than the AI-tooling gap.

**The gap is NOT** in European VC-backed startups needing brand/UX work; that market is well served and saturated. **The gap is NOT** in CEE pricing alone — Polish/Czech tier-1 are at the same band.

## 9. Strategic implications for RS

1. **Buyer-funnel priority:** Series A/B AI startups in Paris/Berlin/London (cluster 1) + cross-border fintech needing CEE/MENA localisation (cluster 5) + diaspora founders (cluster 5).
2. **Geographic outreach priority:** **Paris > London for AI** (Mistral/H/Poolside ecosystem = founder referral access). London for fintech only.
3. **MD SRL stays.** No EU regime cleanly beats 7% MITP. Romanian citizenship + optional PFA layer = optimal personal optionality.
4. **CEE pricing wedge does NOT work in isolation.** Sell wedge bundle: vertical depth + AI-native + founder-direct + diaspora-bilingual.
5. **C-model with public AI tooling is the real EU gap** — Forge/Muse must be SHIPPABLE artifacts (docs, repos, changelog), not just internal tools. Otherwise wedge collapses in 6-12 months as competitors copy.
6. **Avoid dead/dying companies.**
