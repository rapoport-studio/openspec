## Background

The 2026-05-13 desk-research synthesis examined the spec-driven development ecosystem across four dimensions:

1. **Tool ecosystem** — which tools use "spec-driven" or adjacent vocabulary; how mature is tooling for spec-code traceability.
2. **Buyer vocabulary** — whether enterprise buyers recognize "spec-driven development" as a distinct service category vs. conflating with BDD/TDD/agile ceremonies.
3. **Competitor positioning** — do any competitors in MD/RO/IL/EU markets differentiate on methodology transparency or openspec-like artifacts.
4. **Differentiation window** — how long the methodology-transparency wedge holds before it becomes standard practice.

The synthesis produced the raw findings that this inquiry will formalize into promoted, confidence-rated claims during Phase 2 vesting.

## Open questions

1. Is "spec-driven development" the right buyer-facing vocabulary, or does the buyer hear "documentation" and discount it?
2. What is the shortest falsifiable test for "methodology transparency is a differentiator" — a sales conversation log, a win/loss analysis, or a content-engagement metric?
3. Does the competitive gap close faster because large consultancies adopt SDD practices, or because tooling commoditizes them?
