> **Status:** point-in-time research register, snapshot of Israeli high-tech market and competitive set as of May 2026. Will age — facts have ~6–12 month shelf life. Most load-bearing structural fact: **Israel is a product economy, not a services economy** — the analysis frame is "who's a buyer / who's a competitor / who's neither", not "who would compete with us locally."

## TL;DR — Israel tech market in numbers

- **High-tech output 2024:** NIS 317B (~$87B) = **17.3% of GDP** — largest single sector but stagnating two years running.
- **Workforce:** 391K in 2024 (11.5% of national workforce); H1 2025 rose to 403K. **First decline in a decade in 2024** (-1.2%).
- **VC funding 2024:** $12.2B (+31% YoY adj). Peak was $27B in 2021; trough $4.3–8.3B in 2023. **2025: $15.6B (AI-driven concentration).**
- **Average gross tech salary:** NIS 32,300 (~$8,720/мес) — 2.8× national average.
- **War impact:** ~28K tech workers (~7%) called as reservists Oct 2023; 8,300 relocated abroad ≥1 year; 70% of firms reported operating difficulties. **Operational, not paralyzed.**
- **Tax:** Corporate 23% standard; **Preferred Tech Enterprise 12%** central / **7.5% periphery (Development Area A)**; Special PTE (revenue ≥NIS 1B in NIS 10B group) 6%/8%/5%; **Angels Law 2023–2026** gives 25–30% credit on up to NIS 4M angel cheques.
- **Strategic conclusion for RS:** Israel is **referral-or-nothing**. Cold outreach near-zero conversion. Address a narrow buyer cohort (Section C AI startups + post-2022 RU-aliyah founders) via inside-partner only, OR skip for 2026.

**Sector composition (2024 funding-weighted):**
- **Cybersecurity:** 38% of all tech funding ($4.0B), 7% of companies. >20% of global private cyber investment.
- **AI/ML:** $2.4B captured 2024.

**Major hubs:**
- **Tel Aviv:** ~70% of national VC; densest startup cluster globally per km²; SaaS, fintech, generalist.
- **Jerusalem:** ~13% of funding; biotech, life sciences, deeptech, enterprise software (Hebrew U).
- **Haifa:** ~8.5%; semiconductors, robotics, autonomous vehicles, hardware (Technion); NVIDIA new R&D campus at Kiryat Tivon.
- **Be'er Sheva:** ~3%; cybersecurity + AI hub (Ben-Gurion U + IDF cyber units relocated).

## 2. War impact (Oct 7, 2023 onward) — precise numbers

This section is load-bearing because it materially affects whether Israel is a market RS should pursue right now.

- **National mobilization:** 360K reservists called Oct 2023 (~4% of total population).
- **Tech-sector reservists:** **~28,000 high-tech workers called up = ~7% of the tech workforce** (CTech / IIA). Tech workers were **2× over-represented** in reserves (~20% of all reservists vs. ~11.5% of workforce).
- **Some firm-level estimates ranged 15–30%** of staff out at peak — the 7% is the sector-aggregate floor; firm-level pain higher.
- **70% of tech firms reported operating difficulties** ([IIA survey](https://www.timesofisrael.com/about-70-of-tech-firms-face-operating-difficulties-amid-reservists-call-up/)).
- **Work hours -7% in October 2023** sector-wide.
- **Brain drain:** 8,300 tech workers relocated abroad for ≥1 year in first 9 months post-Oct 7 (~2% of workforce). ([Globes](https://en.globes.co.il/en/article-8300-tech-employees-left-israel-after-start-of-war-1001507226))
- **VC sentiment:** Q4 2023 funding cratered; recovery began H1 2024, accelerated late 2024.
- **Continued reserve burden:** mid-2025, IDF preparing 450K-soldier call-ups; reserve duty roughly 2× budgeted volume.

**Net read for RS:** war is real-but-bounded drag. Tech sector kept growing output through it, recovered VC by 2024, but employment shrank in 2024 and emigration is structural. **Israel is operational.** Periphery (Be'er Sheva, north) carries asymmetric risk. Tel Aviv works as usual. **Budgets did tighten** (potential opening for cheaper alternatives), **but buyers became more cautious about foreign dependencies** (potential closing). Net-neutral.

## 3. Tax regime (2024–2025)

- **Standard CIT:** 23%
- **Preferred Technological Enterprise (PTE):** 12% in central Israel (Tel Aviv, Jerusalem) / **7.5% in Development Area A** (Negev, Galilee, periphery) — requires "preferred income" from R&D activity
- **Special Preferred Technological Enterprise (SPTE):** annual revenue ≥ NIS 1B and group revenue ≥ NIS 10B — **6% nationwide** (5%/8% A/non-A split for 10 years)
- **Preferred Enterprise** (industrial, not tech): 7.5% / 16%
- **Angels Law (Knowledge-Intensive Industry Encouragement Law):** in force **25 Jul 2023 → 31 Dec 2026**; 25–30% tax credit on individual angel investments up to NIS 4M into qualifying R&D companies; capital-gains deferral on rolled-over investments; investor must be <25% holder pre-investment.

**Implication for RS:** Israeli PTE 12% requires R&D activity inside Israel + residency. Setting up an Israeli entity makes sense **only if** specific contracts demand local presence. **Default is to keep MD SRL** (7% MITP > Israeli 12%/7.5%).

## 6. Russian-speaking layer — the wedge investigation

Initial hypothesis: large RU-aliyah cohort = large RU-tech studio scene = wedge for us. **Reality is narrower than expected.**

- **1990s aliyah baseline:** ~1M post-Soviet immigrants (~15% of Israeli population at the time); 100K engineers including ~30% of all engineers and ~45% of electronics engineers in Israel by late 1990s — **backbone of original Silicon Wadi**.
- **Post-2022 wave:** ~84,000 Russians + 15,000 Ukrainians since Ukraine invasion (2022 alone: 45,000 Russians vs. 7,700 in 2021). ([Times of Israel](https://www.timesofisrael.com/russian-immigration-streams-in-as-israel-predicts-uptick-in-western-newcomers/))
- **Notable corporate:** Yandex CEO Arkady Volozh relocated; ~800 Yandex devs targeted Israel; Yandex overseas headcount +185% in 2022 (~90% Russian émigrés). ([Haaretz](https://www.haaretz.com/israel-news/tech-news/2022-03-09/ty-article/fearing-sanctions-russias-yandex-wants-to-relocate-workers-to-israel/0000017f-e292-d38f-a57f-e6d2023a0000))

**Russian-speaking studios in Israel (the honest gap-finding):** searches in Russian surfaced almost no boutique product/design studios. RU-language Israeli web layer is dominated by SMB-tier web shops, not VC-startup-grade studios.

- **TRILMO** ([trilmo.net](http://trilmo.net/)) — Tel Aviv web studio, 7+ years, generalist web work
- **PDG Studio** ([pdgstudio.net](https://pdgstudio.net/)) — pan-Israel SMB web shop (since 2002)
- **ADVT Media** ([advt.co.il](https://www.advt.co.il/)) — RU-language internet advertising + web
- **Koda** ([il.koda-ltd.com](http://www.il.koda-ltd.com/)) — graphic + web design
- **BORISSTUDIO** — interior design (not relevant)
- **Sergey Ieffe Design Studio** — listed on Orbita

There is a **Russian-speaking-founder-but-English-marketing layer** (stronger): many founders of EN-marketed boutique studios are from post-1990s aliyah community (e.g. Boguslavsky at Studio Koniak), but they don't market in Russian.

**Implication for RS:** there is NO Israeli RU-language *product/AI* studio that is a peer to RS. The wedge is real **if RS specifically targets RU-speaking founders inside Israel's post-2022 aliyah cohort** — they're in their first 2–3 years outside local HE-networks and want a culturally-fluent partner. **NOT a "RU-marketing for the whole Israeli market" wedge** — that doesn't exist.

## 7. Strategic implications for RS

1. **Israel is referral-or-nothing.** Founder networks tight (Shashua in 3 companies, Wininger in 2). One referenced engagement opens doors; cold outreach to Tel Aviv Slack channels = near-zero conversion.
2. **GTM into Israel = referral-or-nothing.** Either find inside-partner first or skip Israel for 2026.
3. **Don't position as "Israeli agency from Moldova".** Lose on proximity, language, references. **Position as "US/EU-grade boutique with CEE-pricing and AI-tooling fluency, selling operating model not deliverables".** Sell Forge/Muse as peer-products, not as backend.
4. **Realistic addressable audience:** Section C VC-backed AI startups (8 named) + post-2022 RU-aliyah founders. NOT Wix, Monday, Mobileye.
5. **Citizenship/tax:** Israeli PTE gives 12% (or 7.5% periphery), but requires "preferred income from R&D activity" + residency. **Default keeps MD SRL** — 7% MITP still better. Israel = only for specific contracts.
6. **War effect = neither plus nor minus.** Net-neutral, nothing to overweight in strategy.
7. **First buyer to chase if any:** Wonderful ($285M raised in 8 months, AI customer service agents, no design system) — exemplar Section C profile, exactly the gap.
