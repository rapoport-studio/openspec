## Purpose

This thread documents the open questions and session-by-session observations for cohort 1 of the
studio's specialist onboarding. The founding cohort is the first empirical test of:

1. The `multi-author-canvas-instructions-template.md` six-block shape in production.
2. Whether Block B course-meta lands as helpful or condescending (per `value-attribution.md § Q4`
   first live instance).
3. Whether Block D live-ratification mitigates the pre-analyzed-team risk (per
   `openspec-block-authorship.md`).
4. The training curriculum (Axis A + B) as an onboarding mechanism — is the bar calibrated
   correctly?

This is a research thread, not an operational log. Observations are recorded as they happen;
conclusions are appended after Sessions 1–4 complete, not after each session.

## Q-FC-* register

Open questions specific to the founding cohort. Answers are recorded in `## Answers` sub-sections
as they close.

### Q-FC-1 — Does Block B land as helpful or condescending?

**Source:** `value-attribution.md § Q4 first live instance`; `multi-author-canvas-instructions-template.md § Block B`.

**What we are watching:** after Loop 0 of Session 1, does the cohort 1 specialist report (or
demonstrate in their canvas turns) that the course-meta directive oriented them, or did it feel
like being handed a manual before being allowed to speak?

**Signal to collect:** specialist's unprompted engagement with the Block B framing vs. ignoring it.
Whether their first authoring turn follows the Block B structure or bypasses it.

**Resolves at:** end of Session 1.

---

### Q-FC-2 — Does Block D live-ratification mitigate the pre-analyzed-team risk?

**Source:** `openspec-block-authorship.md § Pre-analyzed team risk`.

**What we are watching:** the risk is that Block D (intake-derived) is filled before the first
session by Pavel, which means the specialist is handed a description of themselves they did not
write. Does the Loop 0 ratification step (specialist reads Block D and corrects it) actually
reduce the felt-imposition of the pre-filled description?

**Signal to collect:** whether the specialist edits Block D during Loop 0; whether they reference
it in later turns as their own framing or as Pavel's framing.

**Resolves at:** end of Session 1 (Loop 0 outcome).

---

### Q-FC-3 — Does Muse adapt to the specialist's stated vocabulary?

**Source:** `multi-author-canvas-instructions-template.md § Block D § Vocabulary kind`.

**What we are watching:** Block D includes a `vocabulary kind` field naming the specialist's
preferred framing (e.g. "writes in market-structure terms"; "uses hypothesis-first framing"). Does
Muse's output in Sessions 2–3 reflect this adaptation, or does it revert to its base register?

**Signal to collect:** whether the specialist corrects Muse's vocabulary more than once in the
same session (failure signal) vs. fewer corrections over time (adaptation signal).

**Resolves at:** end of Session 3.

---

### Q-FC-4 — Does the specialist feel valued vs. over-managed?

**Source:** `value-attribution.md § Q4`.

**What we are watching:** the risk is that the six-block template, the training curriculum, and
the explicit certification track feel like bureaucratic gatekeeping rather than a genuine
professional relationship.

**Signal to collect:** specialist's self-report (if volunteered); their engagement depth in
Session 4 relative to Session 1 (increasing depth = feeling respected; plateau or withdrawal =
over-management signal).

**Resolves at:** end of Session 4.

---

### Q-FC-5 — Is the Axis A training brief well-calibrated?

**Source:** `openspec/_training/openspec-authoring-axis-a.md § 3 (the training brief)`.

**What we are watching:** the fictional brief ("Add specialist availability calendar") was designed
to be small, self-contained, and implementable without live-system access. Is it the right size
for a first training change — not trivially easy, not so complex it requires weeks?

**Signal to collect:** how many revision rounds the specialist needs to reach zero structural
critiques; whether the senior reviewer's feedback clusters around the same patterns (suggesting
the brief itself is ambiguous) or different patterns per round (suggesting the specialist is
iterating correctly).

**Resolves at:** when the specialist's Axis A training change passes review.

---

### Q-FC-6 — Is the Axis B stress-test brief well-calibrated?

**Source:** `openspec/_training/business-model-validation.md § 3`.

**What we are watching:** the three proposals used for the Axis B stress-test (selected by the
senior reviewer from the archive) — are they well-matched to a first-time validator, or do they
require too much context about specific engagements?

**Signal to collect:** whether the specialist can write a structured critique with a specific
unit-economics floor without extensive guiding questions from the senior reviewer.

**Resolves at:** when the specialist's Axis B stress-test passes review.

---

### Q-FC-7 — Does the founding cohort experience surface the correct P0.2 trigger?

**Source:** `design.md § Phase 0 outcomes P0.2`; `define-research-domain-entity/` (deferred).

**What we are watching:** P0.2 chose Variant B (markdown-first research substrate). The trigger
for revisiting (opening `re-open-research-domain-entity`) is concrete markdown pain: concurrent-
writer conflicts, lost attribution, queries that grep cannot answer. Does Sessions 1–4 produce
any of these pain signals?

**Signal to collect:** whether the specialist and Pavel ever need a research file at the same
time; whether session notes reference each other clearly enough without a shared entity; whether
the founder ever cannot find a session observation using `rg`.

**Resolves at:** end of Session 4 (or earlier if a concrete trigger appears).

---

### Q-FC-8 — Vocabulary: does "Specialist" land correctly for the founding cohort?

**Source:** `design.md § Phase 0 outcomes P0.1` (vocabulary lock); `proposal.md § Vocabulary note`.

**What we are watching:** P0.1 chose "extend Specialist" as the default persona term. The
founding cohort's primary mandate may feel more research-oriented than "Specialist" suggests
(the founder's colloquial term was "контрибьютор"). Does the specialist themselves use
"specialist" naturally, or do they reach for different language?

**Signal to collect:** how the specialist describes their own role in canvas turns (unprompted).
Whether the onboarding vocabulary feels aligned with their self-conception.

**Resolves at:** end of Session 2 (vocabulary self-description usually surfaces in the first two
sessions of a new collaboration).

## Session protocol

Per `design.md § 7.3 (add-specialist-onboarding)`, Sessions 1–4 are the empirical test bed.

**Session 1 — Loop 0 + first authoring turn:**
- Observe Q-FC-1 (Block B), Q-FC-2 (Block D ratification).
- Append observation within 24 hours of session.

**Session 2 — First full authoring cycle:**
- Observe Q-FC-3 (vocabulary adaptation), Q-FC-8 (persona vocabulary self-description).
- Append observation within 24 hours.

**Session 3 — Second authoring cycle:**
- Observe Q-FC-3 (continued), Q-FC-5 or Q-FC-6 if training has begun.
- Append observation within 24 hours.

**Session 4 — Review cycle:**
- Observe Q-FC-4 (felt-value), Q-FC-7 (P0.2 trigger), Q-FC-5/Q-FC-6 if applicable.
- Append observation within 24 hours.
- After Session 4: write `## Conclusions` section summarising which Q-FC-* closed, which remain
  open, and whether any trigger a new change folder.
