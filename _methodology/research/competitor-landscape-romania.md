## TL;DR — Romania IT market in numbers

- **ICT sector revenue 2024:** **€23.6B** (€17.7B IT-only). 13% nominal / **~7% real growth — slowest year in a decade**.
- **IT&C share of GDP:** 6.67% (2024, down from 6.8% in 2023). Total economic impact ~€45B.
- **Workforce:** ~196,000 IT specialists; avg gross salary >15,000 lei/мес (~€3,015) — 2.8× national average.
- **Layoffs 2024–2025:** 5,000+ documented (Amazon Iași, Microsoft, Oracle, Bitdefender, Cognizant). First contraction in 10+ years.
- **Tax regime:** **IT income-tax exemption KILLED 1 Jan 2025** (OUG 156/2024 "Train Ordinance"). Standard 10% PIT applies. **Not reinstated.**
- **Direct competitive set:** real and material — **Linnify** (Cluj) is the clearest peer to RS's hybrid C-model, plus Deduxer (AI-augmented delivery), RebelDot (FT1000 #1 RO IT 2026, ~€22M).
- **Strategic conclusion for RS:** take Romanian citizenship for EU mobility, **but keep corporate structure in Moldova** — MITP 7% is now structurally better than any Romanian regime.

### Tax regime — current state (May 2026)

**The IT income-tax exemption is dead.**

- **OUG 156/2024 ("Train Ordinance"), published 31 Dec 2024**, abrogated the IT income-tax exemption effective Jan 2025 salaries. The exemption that ran for 20+ years (0% PIT on gross salary up to 10,000 lei/мес for staff at firms with CAEN codes 5821, 5829, 6201, 6202, 6209) is gone. ([Noerr](https://www.noerr.com/en/insights/romania-amendments-to-income-tax-exemption-for-the-it-industry), [ZRVP](https://zrvp.ro/the-impact-of-the-train-ordinance-on-romanias-it-sector/), [Kinstellar](https://www.kinstellar.com/news-and-insights/detail/3135/important-fiscal-changes-in-romania-starting-january-2025))
- **What applies now to IT employees:** standard **10% flat PIT**, full **CAS (25%)** and **CASS (10%)**. The reduced CAS of 20.25% (with 4.25% Pillar II opt-out) IT staff had — gone for IT.
- **Public sector:** Min Finance announced public-sector IT staff would get the same fiscal facilities as private — but this was **before** OUG 156/2024 abolished the facility for everyone; effectively moot.
- **Corporate side still favorable:** standard CIT 16%; the **microîntreprindere regime survives** (1% on revenue with employees, up to ~€500k turnover threshold — being tightened).
- **Net effect:** net pay for a 10,000-lei IT salary dropped roughly 11–13%. This is what's driving relocations to Moldova IT Park (7% flat on turnover, locked until 2035) — MITP confirms Romania and Ukraine top its inbound country list.

## 2. Top players in three tiers

**Tier 1 (3,000+ headcount RO):** All foreign captives. None compete on Romanian-soil branding.

- **Amazon Development Center Romania** — ~3,400 (2024), down from peak 4,200 (2022). Captive product engineering (Alexa, AWS, Ring). ([Romania Insider](https://www.romania-insider.com/amazon-development-center-iasi-layoffs-2025))
- **IBM Romania** — 3,307 (2024). Consulting + outsourced enterprise dev + cloud. ([economedia.ro](https://economedia.ro/cei-mai-importanti-angajatori-din-it-din-romania-au-renuntat-la-sute-de-salariati-intr-un-an-compania-care-conducea-topul-din-2023-a-ramas-fara-1000-de-angajati-in-2024.html))

## 4. Where the gap is in Romania

Honest assessment: **gap is narrower than in Moldova**. Romania has:
- Linnify already claiming "agentic AI" + validation-driven development at scale
- Deduxer already doing AI-augmented delivery + shipped product
- Tapptitude/Wolfpack established at upper boundary
- Strong brand-only studios (ARMEANU, Brandient, Colorblind) credibly serving founder clients

**What is actually under-served in Romania:**

1. **The 1–3 person AI-native full-app studio that ships its own meta-tooling as a brand** — Linnify has Linny but doesn't market it as a peer product; Deduxer ships Accordio but it's an end-user SaaS, not internal infra. **Publishing your toolchain as a positioning move is open territory.**
2. **Trilingual (RO/RU/EN) coverage for Eastern-Europe-fluent founders** — RO studios market EN-only. The RU-language layer is uncontested in the legitimate-studio segment. **This is where Moldovan provenance becomes a genuine asset, not a disadvantage.**
3. **Founder-pair + spec-driven methodology brand** — ARMEANU owns this in pure brand, Product Rocket owns it in pure design. **Nobody owns it in dev+design+AI.**
4. **Public pricing in the dev+AI segment** — ARMEANU does it for brand, nobody does it for product engineering. Differentiator if RS wants it.

**Real saturation areas to avoid:** generic "Webflow studio," "branding agency," "mobile app product partner" — three to five solid players in each, no room.

## 5. Strategic implications for RS

1. **Take Romanian citizenship — but keep the company in Moldova.** RO IT-льгота dead; MITP 7% structurally better. EU passport + MD-структура = optimum. **RO PFA** (persoana fizica autorizată) optional as light EU-billing layer for specific RO contracts.
2. **Linnify is the main competitor reference.** Study them in detail: how they sell, how they present Linny, what's their pricing, their case-studies. They proved C-model works. **Our wedge: public methodological IP** (OpenSpec, open tools) **+ trilingual + founder-pair signature.** All things Linnify doesn't do.
3. **Trilingual EN/RO/RU is the only unoccupied niche** where Moldovan provenance is competitive advantage. Eastern-European founders with RU-fond — under-covered audience with real purchasing power.
4. **RO-clients are a real pool.** After exemption was killed, Romanian mid-size companies lost cost-edge. They need leverage. AI-augmented engagement on "shipped output" model — could land precisely now.
5. **Geographic strategy:** Cluj is the main competitive cluster (Linnify, Tapptitude, Wolfpack, Floating Dots). **Bucharest and Timișoara less saturated** in our category. If building on-ground presence — Bucharest more rational.
