## 1. Why we did this research

Pavel asked: *what's the competitive landscape for Rapoport Studio across MD/RO/IL/EU; what services do they sell; how do they monetize; who are their clients; where are the gaps; and what does this mean for our home page and monetization story?*

This research's value is **not knowledge for its own sake — it's reduction of wrong moves**. Without it:
- We assume Linnify doesn't exist → enter market thinking we're first → real positioning shock
- We assume Romania has IT tax exemption → register Romanian SRL → discover it's gone → costly mistake
- We assume Israel is open to cold outreach → burn months → discover referral-or-nothing
- We assume CEE pricing wedge is large → underprice → undercut by Polish shops who are cheaper anyway

**Research has 6-12 month shelf life.** Re-running pan-region competitor research without strong reason adds noise, not signal. Four regions surveyed May 2026.

## 3. Decisions now made (locked, no further research needed)

### A. Corporate vehicle

**MD SRL stays.** No EU regime cleanly beats Moldova's MITP 7% (locked to 2035).

- Estonia OÜ — best for reinvested profit only (22% on distributions, preferred 14% abolished Jan 2025)
- Cyprus IP Box — 2.5%→3% from Jan 2026; comparable for IP holding only
- Romania — IT income-tax exemption killed Jan 2025
- Ireland — 12.5% CIT, tightened
- Israel PTE — 12% / 7.5% periphery, requires residency + R&D activity

**Rationale**: see [`competitor-landscape-europe.md`](./competitor-landscape-europe.md) § 2 and [`competitor-landscape-romania.md`](./competitor-landscape-romania.md) § 1.

### B. Romanian citizenship — yes, take it

Adds personal optionality (Schengen + EU work rights) and lightweight EU billing layer (Romanian PFA: 10% PIT + 25% CAS + 10% CASS, **VAT-exempt to RON 395K = ~€78K** as of Sep 2025; **EU Small Business Scheme to €100K EU-wide** via Directive 2020/285) **alongside the SRL — does not replace it.**

### C. ICP — Muse-first founder-track

Out of three options (A: Studio leverage / B: Platform-first / C: Hybrid network capture), **C** was already chosen earlier in the research thread. The four regional reports lock-in further:

- All three buyer-cohorts (RO Series A/B, IL Section C, EU AI Series A/B) are **founders on early stage needing architectural/spec interlocutor.** Exactly Muse Architect mode.
- Forge-for-network is a working product, but our network is internal — we don't yet have 100+ specialists to sell it as SaaS. P2 after founder traction.
- Linnify (RO) and Muze (IL) have already proved this model works on founder-track.
- Diaspora-bilingual wedge works only with founders, not with specialist hiring.

### D. Geographic outreach priority

1. **Paris > London for AI** (Mistral/H/Poolside ecosystem = founder referral access)
2. **Berlin** for AI/SaaS Series A/B
3. **London** for fintech only
4. **Israel** = referral-or-nothing — only if inside-partner found, otherwise skip 2026
5. **Cluj/Warsaw/Prague** — competitive cluster, not buyer cluster

### E. Trilingual EN/RO/RU positioning is mandatory

Single unoccupied wedge across all four regions:
- Moldova: Livada is EN-only (correct), but no one trilingual
- Romania: all studios market EN-only
- Israel: no RU-language product/AI studio at boutique tier
- Europe: no studio bridges CEE founder networks to Western capital trilingually

Moldovan provenance becomes asset, not disadvantage. Diaspora founder networks (RO/IL/MD) are the moat for Chișinău-based studio.

### F. Avoid-list (don't waste time)

**Dead/dying:** Northvolt (bankrupt March 2025), Gorillas (closed May 2024), TIER Mobility (sunset), Stability AI (weakened bailout), Just Eat Takeaway (Prosus acquisition), Adevinta (private equity), Deliveroo (DoorDash).

**Closed-shop / not buyers for us:** Wix (runs own agency platform), Monday, Fiverr (structural threat to low-end agencies anyway), Booking, Spotify, SAP, ASML, Adyen, Check Point, NICE, Mobileye, Tower Semi, Amdocs.

## 5. What this research does NOT do

Be honest about limits:

- **Does not generate leads.** Only suggests who to write to.
- **Does not write our homepage copy.** Only says ICP is founders, not specialists.
- **Does not replace pitching.** Knowing about Section C is 1% of work; finding warm intro to Wonderful or Decart is 99%.
- **Has 6-12 month shelf life.** Linnify will change pricing, Mistral will raise another round, EU AI Act enters full force — report ages.

After these four regional reports, **research has diminishing returns**. A fifth regional report (Balkans? Turkey?) is no longer needed — adds noise, not signal. **Time to convert insights to action.**

## 6. Recommended next-step deep-dive (deferred)

A focused 5-peer monetization teardown is the highest-ROI next research, **deferred per Pavel's request to focus on home page development first**. When ready:

**Top 5 candidates for monetization deep-dive:**

1. **Linnify** (Cluj, RO) — closest direct match across all axes. Mandatory.
2. **Muze** (Tel Aviv, IL) — closest IL peer. Founder-pair, AI Architecture as a Service. Mandatory.
3. **Mozza** (Paris, FR) — boutique scale + AI-integration + product design in Paris (AI-hub).
4. **Maison Meta** (Paris/London/NY) — "first generative AI agency", own AI-stack + services. Vertical luxury, but monetization mechanics relevant.
5. **Set Studio** (Andy Bell, UK distributed) — micro-team methodology-as-positioning, all public. Reference for minimum operational model.

For each, a "monetization teardown" should document:
- Pricing model (project / hourly / retainer / equity / hybrid)
- Disclosed prices if any
- Engagement size bands (small / medium / large)
- Service catalog (productized vs bespoke)
- AI-tooling monetization (free / freemium / paid SaaS / internal-only)
- Sales channel (referrals / content / outbound / conferences / partnerships)
- Marketing surface (blog, podcast, twitter, case-studies, methodology docs, conference talks)
- Founder narrative on website
- Hiring patterns (LinkedIn — what roles, growth rate)
- Geographic / language footprint
- Cycle time (how fast they ship a typical project)

Plus **1 buyer-cohort deep-dive** (e.g. Wonderful — $285M in 8 months, no design system) to produce a ready-to-go outreach package.

## 7. What's open / not yet decided

Things this research does NOT settle:

1. **Monetization mechanics for Muse and Forge as SKUs.** ICP is founders, but exact SKU shape (per-canvas / per-seat / per-engagement / equity) — not yet locked.
2. **Pricing floor / engagement-size bands** for RS itself. Approximate ranges from research:
   - Series A/B AI startup brand+site: €40-120K
   - Mid-cap SaaS B2B redesign: €60-200K
   - Deep-tech rebrand milestone: €80-250K
   - Tel Aviv senior product designer hourly: $120-180
3. **What we open-source vs keep proprietary** in Forge/Muse stack. Public surface needed (per insight #8) — but how much.
4. **Inside-partner identification for Israel** — without one, IL is skip-2026.
5. **Pavel's personal time allocation across hubs** — Romania (citizenship + Linnify-area), Paris (AI hub), or remote-only.
6. **Exact homepage block additions** — Tools-block, double-CTA structure, language switcher prominence — these go into the change proposal that follows this research.
